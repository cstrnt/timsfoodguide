Dim Sum Palace is not trying to impress you. This is, paradoxically, what makes it impressive.

The room seats perhaps a hundred and twenty. On Sunday morning at half past ten it is completely full, and the noise level is something you must simply accept as part of the experience. Families have occupied corner tables for the duration. Carts are pushed past with an authority that suggests they will not be stopping for anything. Tea is poured into your cup before you've finished reading the menu, which is fine because you don't need the menu.

This is the right way to eat here: point at things. Ask questions if you want to but don't require answers to make decisions. Keep the table clear when you finish a basket. Order more than you think you need.

## What to order

The har gow are the test. Thin translucent skin, tightly pleated — I counted nine pleats on one, which is excessive in the best way — yielding to a shrimp filling that is cold-water sweet and properly seasoned. These alone justify the trip.

The siu mai: pork and shrimp, a little ginger, a dot of roe on top that turns out to be more than decorative. The cheung fun with BBQ pork is soft and silky and comes in a sauce that is slightly sweet and thoroughly addictive. The turnip cake is pan-fried and arrives crisp on two sides. The egg tarts come at the end and are given the gravity they deserve.

I'd also strongly recommend whatever the kitchen is sending around in the unlabeled steamers. I have never had a bad one.

## The experience

Service is fast and functional. "Warm" is not the word I'd use, but "efficient" undersells it — there's a competence to the whole operation that is its own form of hospitality. You are never waiting. You are never crowded. The pace of the meal is set for you and it is the right pace.

The one drawback is the wait: no reservations before noon on weekends, and the queue forms. Arrive before opening or plan for twenty minutes on the pavement. Bring someone to talk to.

## On value

Four people, tea, twelve baskets, two plates, dessert: €55. I have paid more for worse in every other context. This is not up for debate.
