Some restaurants are discoveries. This one felt more like being let in on something that had been there all along, waiting.

Phở Thành Long has been in the same spot in Wedding for over twenty years. The dining room has not been renovated in at least half that time, possibly more. The chairs are mismatched. The menu is laminated and printed in a font that stopped being current sometime in the early 2000s. None of this is a criticism. All of it is beside the point.

## The pho

The broth. I keep coming back to the broth. It is the kind of thing that requires no embellishment and tolerates no shortcuts: beef bones, charred ginger and onion, star anise, cloves, cinnamon, and time. A lot of time. The result is clear and dark amber and deeply savory without being heavy — there's a sweetness to it that only comes from proper caramelization of the bones, not from sugar.

The noodles arrive separately in a dry bowl; you pour the broth over them tableside. This matters. It keeps the noodles from overcooking in transit and means your first spoonful is at the right temperature. The accompanying plate of herbs, bean sprouts, lime and chili is generous.

I order the combination: rare sliced beef that finishes cooking in the hot broth, and brisket that has been simmered until it yields without falling apart. The brisket alone would be worth coming for.

## Everything else

There is a spring roll starter that I've had twice. It's good, but the pho is what you're here for and the portions are large enough that starting with something else is a commitment.

The tea is complimentary and comes without being asked.

## The honest part

The vibe is strictly functional. Nobody is going to ask how your evening is going or suggest a wine pairing. The room fills quickly at lunch and the turnover is brisk. You are there to eat, and the food understands this assignment completely.

At €11 for a large bowl that fills you until dinner, the value is not up for discussion.
