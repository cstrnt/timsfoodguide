There are restaurants you discover by accident and ones you seek out on a tip. Bun & Board was the latter — a name that kept surfacing whenever the ramen conversation came up in this city. After three visits, I understand why.

The space fits maybe twenty people. No reservations, no promises. You get there early or you wait, which in January means standing in the cold watching steam rise from a bowl through the window. It's worth it.

## The broth

The tonkotsu takes eighteen hours. You can taste it — dense, porky, with a depth that most places in this city can't fake. There's a clarity to it, too; it coats the spoon but doesn't feel heavy. The kitchen adds a drizzle of black garlic oil right before service that cuts through just enough to keep things interesting.

## The noodles

Hand-pulled, with the kind of irregular bite that tells you someone was actually paying attention. They don't overcook them, which sounds basic but apparently isn't.

## The rest

Chashu: properly braised, not just blanched. Soft-boiled egg: exactly six minutes, which I know because I asked. A bamboo shoot situation that I usually find unremarkable but here added something.

## The verdict

The price has crept up. A bowl is now €17, which is the kind of thing you notice in Prenzlauer Berg these days. The portion is generous, the quality is real, but value is where I have to be honest: this is a treat, not a Tuesday night fallback.

The vibe is warm but slightly harried when full — the team is small and the room is tight. Service is friendly and knowledgeable but don't expect lingering.

Come for the broth. Leave full.
