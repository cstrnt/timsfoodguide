The word "neighborhood café" has been degraded to the point of uselessness. It now usually means: a place with reclaimed wood furniture, oat milk, and a sourdough toast situation that comes with a tiny glass of orange juice and costs €14. The neighborhood part is aspirational.

Café Morgen is actually what the phrase is supposed to mean. It has been open on the same corner in Friedrichshain for eleven years. The regulars are regular. The owner is usually there. The menu hasn't changed substantially since it opened, which is either a sign of complacency or of confidence, and here it's confidence.

## The eggs

This is the thing to know: the soft scrambled eggs are very good. Not in a revelatory way, not something you'll describe to people who weren't there, but consistently, unshowily, correctly good. They use butter — real butter, more than you'd expect — and take them off the heat early enough that they stay silky rather than dry. Served with whatever bread they have that day, usually sourdough, always good.

The poached eggs are also done properly, which is rarer than it should be. No vinegary water, no rubbery whites.

## The coffee

Single-origin filter, rotated seasonally, from a roaster in Kreuzberg. Espresso drinks made by people who know what they're doing and don't perform it. The cortado is reliably excellent.

## The room

High ceilings, morning light from the east-facing windows, newspapers on wooden dowels that nobody under forty has touched. It gets busy between nine and eleven on weekends but moves through tables quickly. Come on a Tuesday morning and you can sit for two hours without feeling like you're in anyone's way.

## The number that matters

Eggs, toast, a coffee, and a small juice: €13. That figure requires no further commentary.
