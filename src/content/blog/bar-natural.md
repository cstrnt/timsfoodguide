The natural wine bar as a format has become its own kind of cliché — exposed brick, a chalkboard list that changes too often to be useful, plates with three components that cost €18 and leave you hungry. Bar Natural mostly avoids this trap, and the ways it doesn't are easy to forgive.

The room is small, maybe thirty covers, with a zinc-topped bar running along one wall where you can sit and talk to whoever's pouring. The lighting is correctly dim. There's music that doesn't announce itself. These things sound minor and are not.

## The wine

The list skews French and Italian, with a few Spanish and Georgian bottles that reward curiosity. The team knows the wines well — not in the way that means they'll lecture you, but in the way that means they'll ask two questions and then bring you something that fits. I've been steered well here every time.

Bottles range from €32 to north of €100, with enough in the €40–55 range to make an evening easy. By-the-glass pours are honest in size. Skin-contact whites are well-represented if that's your preference; there are also structured reds for those who arrived at natural wine through the back door.

## The food

The kitchen operates at a scale appropriate to its role: this is a wine bar where food is taken seriously, not a restaurant where wine is an afterthought. That's the distinction. Dishes arrive as they're ready and are designed to share.

The burrata with anchovy-spiked salsa verde was the best version of a plate I've had dozens of times. The anchovies cut the richness without dominating; the oil underneath was the kind of thing you drag bread through until you're embarrassed. Speaking of which: the bread is from a bakery down the street and is very good.

Grilled lamb chops with harissa. A small plate of jamón cut thicker than you'd expect. Seasonal vegetables done in a way that suggests someone actually thought about them. Nothing overly ambitious; everything correct.

## The caveat

A glass and two or three plates per person puts you at €40–55. For Neukölln this is on the higher end, which is what pulls the value score down. You're paying partly for the wine knowledge and partly for the room, and both are worth something — just know what you're signing up for.

Reservations essential on weekends. Walk-ins at the bar on weeknights.
